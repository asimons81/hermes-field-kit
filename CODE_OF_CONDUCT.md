# Code of Conduct

## Our standard

We are committed to a collaborative project where people can disagree directly without attacking one another.

Examples of expected behavior include:

- Critiquing ideas, code, documentation, and test evidence rather than people
- Giving actionable feedback
- Respecting privacy and attribution
- Disclosing conflicts of interest
- Accepting maintainer decisions without harassment
- Correcting mistakes publicly when the mistake affected the project publicly

Unacceptable behavior includes harassment, threats, discriminatory language, doxxing, sexualized attention, sustained disruption, impersonation, or publishing private information without permission.

## Scope

This policy applies in repository issues, pull requests, discussions, release spaces, and other public spaces used to represent the project.

## Enforcement

Report conduct concerns privately to the maintainer through the contact method on the maintainer's GitHub profile. Do not open a public issue containing sensitive personal information.

Maintainers may edit or remove content, close or lock conversations, reject contributions, temporarily restrict participation, or permanently ban participation when necessary to protect the project and its participants.

## Attribution

This policy is adapted from the Contributor Covenant, version 2.1, and simplified for a small maintainer-led project.
